My Travel Dream

I’ve always dreamed of traveling abroad to explore new cultures, taste different cuisines, and experience the wonders of the world. Sadly, I haven’t had the opportunity yet. However, I have realized that Indonesia itself offers unforgettable destinations that are equally magical. From mountains covered in mist to beaches glowing with golden sunsets, each trip has given me stories worth keeping. In this section, I’ll share some of the most memorable places I’ve visited in Indonesia, as well as my dream destination abroad. These journeys made me appreciate my homeland while still fueling my dream to step into foreign lands one day.

Dieng Plateau

Visiting Dieng feels like stepping into another world. The air is so cold that even during the day, you can see your breath as if it were winter. Farmers there grow a special type of chili that looks like jalapeños but with its own fiery kick. Beyond that, the colorful Telaga Warna lake and the ancient Hindu temples scattered across the plateau add an almost mystical charm to the region. For me, Dieng is both chilling and enchanting—literally.

Bali

Bali is not only about stunning beaches, but also about its delicious cuisine. One of the most unforgettable meals I had there was the famous babi guling—a roasted pig dish bursting with flavor and spices. But what makes Bali truly unique is how culture and daily life blend together: traditional dance performances in small villages, temple offerings lining the streets, and the warm smiles of locals welcoming every visitor. Every corner of Bali feels alive with tradition and energy.

Dreaming of Japan

If there’s one country abroad I want to visit the most, it’s Japan. The iconic Shibuya Crossing in Tokyo has always fascinated me, especially seeing it in countless movies and videos online. Beyond that, Japanese cuisine—sushi, ramen, and so much more—feels like a dream for my taste buds. I also admire how clean and organized the cities are, making every corner look perfect. Japan, to me, is the ultimate blend of tradition, modernity, and perfection. Someday, I hope to walk its streets in person.
